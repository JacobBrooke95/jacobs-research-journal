About This Journal

What I'm doing here and why.

I'm Jacob Brooke. I work in budget and planning for Missouri state government in Jefferson City. Before I got into the bureaucratic weeds of policy, I studied political science and public administration at Truman State. I'm the kind of person who still keeps a physical bullet journal, tracks his VO2 max obsessively, and has too many tabs open about AI model releases.

This journal is where I write longer pieces — the kind of thing that doesn't fit in a daily note or a quick jot. Research I actually do. Ideas I've sat with. Things I want to understand better by explaining them out loud.

What you'll find here

Mostly AI and technology, because that's what I spend a lot of cycles thinking about right now. But also public systems, productivity infrastructure, the occasional piece on health or running, and whatever I've been reading.

I'm not trying to be a newsletter. I'm not trying to build an audience. I'm trying to think in public a little, because the act of writing something down is the best way I know to figure out if I actually understand it.

The setup

I keep a large Obsidian vault as a second brain — PARA method, thousands of notes going back to 2017. This journal is a small public window into the thinking that happens in there. The vault stays private; the finished thoughts come here.

Get in touch

I'm on GitHub as JacobBrooke95. If something here is wrong or interesting, that's probably the best way to reach me.
