Opening paragraph. Start with something concrete — a thing you noticed, a number that surprised you, a moment of friction. Don't open with "In recent years..." or "It's no secret that..."

Section Heading

Body copy. Write in paragraphs, not bullets. You can use bullets for actual lists of things, but don't use them to avoid writing real sentences.

Use this callout box for an important caveat, key data point, or something the reader really shouldn't miss.

More body copy.

Use blockquotes for actual quotes from people, papers, or documentation — not for your own emphasis. That's what bold is for.

A Sub-section If Needed

Only use H3 if a section genuinely has sub-parts. Don't manufacture hierarchy.

Closing paragraph. Land the piece. What do you actually think? What would you tell a friend who asked about this topic? Don't trail off — end on something.
